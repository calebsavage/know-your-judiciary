We hear a lot about federal judges and the associated political battles over appointment, but have you ever stopped to think about how state and local judges are selected?
The country is vast and diverse-- each state has wide latitude to establish and run its own court system. The result is a hodgepodge of rules and requirements that can differ greatly from state to state. Many areas have minimal or no minimum qualifications for judgeship.
